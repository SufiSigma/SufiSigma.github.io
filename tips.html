Train Smarter

PRO
TIPS

The difference between good and great is what you do between training sessions.

01
🎯
Master Form First

Perfect form over high reps every single time. One clean rep is worth ten sloppy ones. Bad form creates injury, and injury stops all progress. Film yourself to check your form.

02
😴
Rest is Growth

Your muscles grow during sleep, not during training. 7-8 hours every night is your most powerful tool. Skipping rest for more training is the fastest way to go backwards.

03
📈
Progressive Overload

Add 1-2 reps, reduce rest time, or advance the exercise every single week. If nothing changes, nothing changes. Stagnation in training is the same as regression.

04
🔥
Warm Up Always

5-10 minutes of joint circles, light movement and mobility work before every session. Cold muscles tear. Warmed muscles perform. This is non-negotiable every single session.

05
📅
Consistency Wins

Training 3 days a week for an entire year beats 7 days for a month every time. Motivation gets you started. Discipline keeps you going. Show up even when you don't feel like it.

06
🧠
Mind Muscle Connection

Consciously squeeze the muscle you're working during every rep. Don't just go through motions — feel it working. This alone can double your progress over time.

Your body can stand almost anything. It's your mind you have to convince.

— The Foundation of Calisthenics

Fuel Your Progress

NUTRITION BASICS

🥩
Protein

Aim for 1.6-2g per kg of bodyweight daily. Protein repairs torn muscle fibers. Without enough protein, you won't grow no matter how hard you train.

🍚
Carbohydrates

Carbs are your primary fuel. Eat complex carbs like rice, oats and bread around training. Don't fear carbs — fear not having energy to train.

💧
Hydration

Drink 2.5-3 liters of water daily. Dehydration reduces strength by up to 20%. If you're thirsty, you're already dehydrated.

⏱️
Meal Timing

Eat a meal 1-2 hours before training. Have protein within 30-60 minutes after. Consistency in eating habits matters more than perfect timing.

Learn From Mistakes

COMMON MISTAKES

✗

Skipping the basics

Many beginners want to do muscle ups before they can do 10 clean pull ups. Master the foundations first. Every advanced skill is built on basics.

✗

Training every day

More training does NOT equal more results. Rest days are when your muscles rebuild stronger. Overtraining leads to injury and burnout.

✗

Ignoring nutrition

You cannot out-train a bad diet. If you're not eating enough protein, you are leaving 50% of your results on the table no matter how hard you train.

✗

Comparing your progress

Everyone's body responds differently. Focus on beating your own previous records, not someone else's timeline.

✗

Quitting too soon

Real results in calisthenics take 6-12 months of consistent training. Most people quit in the first 4-6 weeks. The ones who stay are the ones who transform.
