Structure Your Training

WORKOUT PLANS

Beginner
Foundation

Build your base. 3 days per week. Focus on form over reps.

  • Standard Push Ups — 3×10
  • Dead Hang — 3×30 sec
  • Negative Pull Ups — 3×5
  • Bench Dips — 3×12
  • Wall Handstand — 3×30 sec
  • Rest 60–90 sec between sets
Intermediate
Strength Build

Progress your skills. 4 days per week. Push your limits.

  • Pull Ups — 4×8
  • Parallel Bar Dips — 4×10
  • Decline Push Ups — 4×12
  • Archer Push Ups — 3×6 each
  • Pike Push Ups — 3×10
  • Rest 90 sec between sets
Advanced
Elite Level

Master the skills. 5 days per week. Consistency is everything.

  • Strict Muscle Ups — 5×5
  • One Arm Push Up — 4×4 each
  • Freestanding Handstand — 5 min
  • Ring Dips — 4×8
  • Pseudo Planche Push Ups — 4×8
  • Rest 2 min between sets
Train Smarter

PRO TIPS

🎯
Master Form First

Perfect form over high reps every time. One clean rep is worth ten sloppy ones. Injury stops all progress.

😴
Rest is Growth

Muscles grow during rest, not during training. 7–8 hours of sleep is your most powerful recovery tool.

📈
Progressive Overload

Increase reps, reduce rest, or advance the exercise each week. Stagnation is regression in disguise.

🥗
Fuel Your Body

Aim for 1.6–2g of protein per kg of bodyweight daily. Your diet decides how fast you progress.

🔥
Warm Up Always

5–10 minutes of joint circles and mobility work before every session. Non-negotiable rule.

📅
Consistency Wins

Training 3 days a week for a year beats 7 days for a month. Show up every week without fail.
